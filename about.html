My name is Adrian Martinez. I'm a former soccer player with 15 years of competitive experience.

My competitive career began at Miami Fútbol Academy Rush, formerly known as Miami Rush Kendall SC and Kendall Soccer Coalition. I played there for six years, spending three of them — U14, U16, and U18 — on their Academy teams, the equivalent of present-day MLS NEXT. During that time, I represented the USA in two tournaments in Portugal, and played in Colombia against the youth teams of Atlético Nacional, Deportivo Independiente Medellín, Envigado F.C., and Águilas Doradas. In the US, I played against teams like the New York Red Bulls, Atlanta United, and FC Dallas.

In college, I played for the Rollins College Men's Soccer Team in Division II's Sunshine State Conference — the toughest D-II conference in the nation. The highlight of my four years playing college soccer was reaching the Elite Eight of the NCAA tournament.

Off the field, I went on to a PhD program before turning toward medicine — I'm currently applying to medical school. The same habit got me through both: paying close, unglamorous attention to detail. That's what this is.
